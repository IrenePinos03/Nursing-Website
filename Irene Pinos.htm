Importance of Immunization on Infants in the Philippines



INTRODUCTION


As the saying goes, "Prevention is better than cure," we all want our children to have a better future and a safe environment. We want them to live their lives at their best. That's why our government implemented immunization, especially for our children, to get rid of or avoid many preventable diseases. To some, it may be a choice, but now it is required to have our children immunized for their sake and for the better future of our respective countries.



What is Immunization?


According to PAHO (Pan American Health Organization) "Immunization is the process whereby a person is made resistant to a disease, typically by the administration of a vaccine. Vaccines stimulate the body’s own immune system to protect the person against indefinite infection or disease."



Why is it Important?


This is because immunization protects against diseases, impairments, and fatalities from Vaccine-preventable Diseases (VPDs) theses are:


• Bacterial Pneumonias

• Rotavirus

• Diarrheal illnesses

• Bacterial meningitis

• Poliomyelitis

• Measles, Mumps and Rubella

• Diphtheria

• Tetanus

• Pertussis

• Hepatitis A and B

• Haemaphilus Influenzae Type B



Immunization in the Philippines


According to the Department of Health (DOH) The Expanded Program on Immunization (EPI) was established in 1976 to ensure that infants/children and mothers have access to routinely recommended infant/childhood vaccines. The Vaccines under the EPI are BCG birth dose, Hepatitis B birth dose, Oral Poliovirus Vaccine, Pentavalent Vaccine, Measles Containing Vaccines (Anti-measles Vaccine, Measles, Mumps, Rubella) and Tetanus Toxoid. In 2014, Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine 13 was included in the routine immunization of EPI. Later in 2016, the EPI will become the National Immunization Program (NIP), where it will include immunizations of other populations, such as senior citizen immunizations, school-age immunizations, and adolescent immunizations.


The NIP aims to immunize 95% of children in the Philippines to the fullest extent possible. According to Maria Rosario Vergeire, the officer-in-charge of the Department of Health (DOH), the indefinable lower percentage result for 2022 is 62.9%.



What are the Types of Vaccines?


In order to make it simple, it will be listed following the 5 W’s questions.


1.

Who: Infants

What: PentaHib Vaccine 0.5 ml Brand: PentaValent Vaccine

Where: Injected Intramuscular in Right Vastus Lateralis

When: 1½, 2½, & 3½ months

Why: Provides protection from 5 life-threatening diseases, which are Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, Hepatitis B and Hib.



2.

Who: Infants

What: Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV) 0.5 ml Brand: Pneumococcal Polysaccharide Conjugate Vaccine

Where: Injected Intramuscular in Left Vastus Lateralis

When: 1½, 2½, & 3½ months

Why: Prevention against Pneumococcal disease like Pneumonia.



3.

Who: Infants

What: Inactive Polio Vaccine (IPV) 0.5 ml Brand: IMOVAX polio

Where: Injected Intramuscular in Left Vastus Lateralis

When: 3 & 9 months

Why: Prevention against severe disease caused by Poliovirus.



4.

Who: Infants

What: Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) 2 drops

Where: By mouth

When: 1½, 2½, & 3½ months

Why: Predominant vaccine to prevent or eradicate Polio.



5.

Who: Infants

What: MR (Measles & Rubella) 0.5 ml Code: (MV0), MMR (Measles, Mumps & Rubella) Code: (MV1 & MV2)

Where: Injected Subcutaneous in Right Arm

When: 6, 9 & 12 months

Why: Prevention against Measles, Mumps, and Rubella.



6.

Who: Infants

What: Bacilli Calmette Guerin (BCG) 0.05 ml

Where: Injected Intradermal in Right Arm

When: Right after birth or up to 1 week after birth

Why: Prevention against childhood Tuberculosis Meningitis and Military disease.
